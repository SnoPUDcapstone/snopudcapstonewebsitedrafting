About the Project

Project Description

The Arlington microgrid project was developed to increase the power generation of the Snohomish County PUD at peak hours and includes a solar panel array and battery system. As a pilot project, the PUD is attempting to reduce the balancing reserve capacity burden placed on the Bonneville Power Authority (BPA). The BPA participates in the Energy Imbalance Market and wants to minimize their imbalance to limit costs and maximize profit from the market. To do this, the PUD needs to develop accurate predictions of PV generation by the solar panels and balance the actual PV generation using the battery, called capacity firming. The PUD currently uses the battery to absorb or inject power into the grid to adjust the power at a constant ramp rate when the PV generation fluctuates, called solar smoothing (Hitachi Method). When the predicted and actual PV generation differs, the BPA makes up the difference in power generation from reserves, but this incurs a cost on the PUD. Additionally, every cycle of the battery degrades the battery slightly, which can be associated with a cost. A successful project will limit the costs from the BPA while also preserving the battery’s life.
